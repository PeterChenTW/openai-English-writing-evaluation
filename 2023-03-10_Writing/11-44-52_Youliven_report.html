IETLS Writing Test Report

Essay Topic

You live near a recreation ground. A local school often brings pupils to the recreation ground to do sports, such as football and cricket, but recently this has caused some problems. Write a letter to the head of the school. In your letter:

  • explain why you enjoy living by the recreation ground
  • describe the recent problems at the recreation ground
  • say what you think the school should do.
You should spend about 20 minutes on this task. Write at least 150 words.

Student Essay

Dear Headmaster,

I am writing to express my appreciation for the use of the recreational ground in our community, as well as my concerns regarding the recent activities of some of your students.

As a resident who frequently uses the recreational facilities, I feel privileged to live near such a beautiful and well-maintained area. It is a valuable resource for the whole community, and I appreciate the efforts of the school to utilize it for physical activities.

However, I have noticed that the field has suffered some damage recently, particularly during wet weather. The students using the area for sports like football and cricket have caused large patches of mud and divots, which have become both unsightly and hazardous.

To resolve this issue, I suggest that the school take responsibility for repairing the damage to the field. Additionally, guidance from school staff may be beneficial to prevent students from causing further damage in the future. Perhaps limiting the number of students who can use the field at one time or providing alternative playing locations on the school ground might be valuable.

I believe that these changes can ensure that the recreational ground remains an accessible and safe environment for everyone in the community to enjoy. Thank you for your attention to this matter.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Suggestions

Suggestions for improvement:

  • Expand on the reasons why the recreational ground is valuable to the community.
  • Provide specific examples of the damage caused by the students.
  • Offer more specific suggestions for how the school can prevent or repair damage to the field.
  • Use a wider range of vocabulary to express ideas more precisely.
  • Vary sentence structure to create a more engaging and dynamic writing style.

Corrections

Here are some corrections:

  • The opening sentence can be improved by specifically addressing the headmaster by name.
  • Use "beautifully" instead of "well-maintained" to emphasize the attractiveness of the area.
  • Instead of saying "It is a valuable resource," use a more specific description such as "The recreational ground provides an ideal location for exercise and relaxation."
  • In the second paragraph, use a more precise verb to describe the damage caused by the students, such as "upturned soil" instead of "divots."
  • Don't use contractions such as "can't" or "doesn't" in formal writing.
  • Double-check for sentence fragments, such as "Perhaps limiting the number of students who can use the field at one time or providing alternative playing locations on the school ground might be valuable."

Dear Mr. Johnson,

I am writing to express my gratitude for the presence of the recreational ground in our locality, and to bring to your attention some issues that have arisen with the use of the area by your school pupils.

As someone who frequently uses the field for exercise and relaxation, I find it to be an integral and valuable component of the community. It is always delightful to see students from your school engaging in sports like football and cricket while utilizing the natural beauty of the field.

However, with the rainy season upon us, the condition of the field has been deteriorating. The repeated use of the area by a large number of students has resulted in significant wear and tear damage, such as holes in the ground and patches of mud. This renders it difficult and unsafe to use the recreational ground for any physical activity.

To rectify this issue, I suggest that your school take responsibility for maintaining the field in good condition. I would be grateful if the school could allocate some funding towards its maintenance, such as resurfacing it with a material that would withstand the inclement weather conditions. Your staff may also be able to help by assessing and repairing the current damage, as well as supervising students to ensure that rules regarding safety and care of the facilities are being adhered to.

I hope that this issue can be resolved soon, as the recreational ground is an irreplaceable asset to the community. Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Words

Here are several useful English words with their meanings and example sentences that can be used for this topic:

Integral (adjective)
necessary or essential to the completeness of a whole
Example: The recreational ground is an integral part of our community.
Deteriorating (adjective)
gradually becoming worse or less
Example: The field's condition has been deteriorating over time.
Wear and Tear (noun)
damage resulting from ordinary use
Example: The wear and tear on the field from regular use has made it difficult to maintain.
Resurface (verb)
restore a surface to a good condition
Example: The field needs to be resurfaced in order to repair the damage caused by heavy usage.
Adhered to (verb phrase)
obeyed or followed, as in rules or guidelines
Example: The students were reminded to adhere to the rules regarding care of the recreational ground.
Supervising (verb)
to oversee, manage or watch over
Example: School staff should supervise the use of the recreational ground to ensure it is not damaged.
Asset (noun)
a useful or valuable thing, person or quality
Example: The recreational ground is a valuable asset to our community.
Valuable (adjective)
something that is of great worth or importance
Example: The recreational ground is a valuable resource that should be properly maintained.
